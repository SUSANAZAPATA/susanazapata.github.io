🏙️ Cities That Shape Colombia

🌄 Medellín – The City of Eternal Spring

Once infamous, today admired. Medellín is a symbol of transformation. Nestled in the Aburrá Valley and surrounded by green mountains, it’s known for its perfect weather, innovation, and warmth.

  • Vibe: Creative, entrepreneurial, full of local pride.
  • Must-experience: The Metrocable ride over the comunas, the stunning botanical garden, and the annual Feria de las Flores.
  • Don’t miss: Bandeja paisa, salsa choke parties, and the murals of Comuna 13.

🏙️ Bogotá – The Heart of the Country

The capital. A chaotic symphony of people, politics, culture, and creativity. Bogotá sits high in the Andes and pulses with diversity from every corner of Colombia.

  • Vibe: Fast-paced, intellectual, urban energy with a poetic soul.
  • Must-experience: La Candelaria district, Monserrate at sunset, and the Gold Museum.
  • Don’t miss: Ajiaco soup, Bogotá’s underground music scene, and Sundays on Ciclovía.

🌊 Barranquilla – Where the Party Never Stops

Barranquilla is rhythm, color, and joy — the birthplace of Shakira and the home of Colombia’s second-largest carnival. This Caribbean city is bold, warm, and full of flavor.

  • Vibe: Festive, coastal, loud, and proud.
  • Must-experience: The Carnival in February, the Malecón del Río, and a night of champeta or salsa.
  • Don’t miss: Arepas de huevo, tropical heat, and people who make you feel at home instantly.

🔥 Cali – The Capital of Salsa

Cali is movement — from the salsa that pulses through its streets to the boldness of its people. It’s one of Colombia’s most culturally rich and ethnically diverse cities.

  • Vibe: Passionate, soulful, rebellious.
  • Must-experience: A salsa class, San Antonio neighborhood, and the iconic Cristo Rey.
  • Don’t miss: Chontaduro with honey and salt, salsa clubs like Zaperoco, and the Cali Fair in December.

🍲 Our Food – Flavors That Tell a Story

If you really want to understand Colombia, start with the food. Colombian cuisine is a reflection of our diversity — a mix of Indigenous, African, Spanish, and Arab influences, shaped by geography and tradition.

🥘 Bandeja Paisa (Antioquia)

A legendary dish from the coffee region. It’s hearty, indulgent, and deeply symbolic of Paisa culture.

  • What’s in it? Red beans, white rice, ground beef, chicharrón, fried egg, plantain, arepa, chorizo, avocado, and sometimes a slice of blood sausage.
  • How it feels: Like a hug from your abuela after a long day.

🍲 Ajiaco (Bogotá and the Andes)

A warm, comforting chicken and potato soup made with three kinds of potatoes and flavored with guasca leaves. Served with avocado, rice, and capers.

When to eat it? On a rainy Bogotá afternoon — or any time you need comfort in a bowl.

🌮 Arepas (Everywhere, but different everywhere)

Arepas are corn-based patties eaten throughout Colombia — but they vary wildly by region.

  • Antioquia: Thin and grilled.
  • Caribbean Coast: Stuffed with cheese, eggs, or meat.
  • Santander: Crunchy and made with corn and pork bits.

Arepas are like Colombians themselves — simple, adaptable, and full of character.

🍌 Sancocho (National dish)

This beloved stew is cooked differently across regions, but always with yuca, plantain, corn, and meat (chicken, fish, beef, or pork).

It’s best shared — sancocho is a dish for family gatherings, birthdays, or a lazy Sunday in the countryside.

🧁 Postres (Desserts)

Colombians love sweet things! Some of our most cherished desserts include:

  • Arequipe: Our version of dulce de leche.
  • Obleas: Thin wafers filled with arequipe, cheese, and jam.
  • Brevas con arequipe: Figs with caramel.
  • Natilla y buñuelos: Traditional Christmas treats that bring the whole family together.
